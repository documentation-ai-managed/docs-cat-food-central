## What is Animal Plasma?

Animal plasma is the liquid component of blood that remains after red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets are removed through centrifugation.

This protein-rich fluid, composed of essential compounds like albumin, globulin, and fibrinogen, has gained significant attention in the pet food industry, particularly in dry food formulations, due to its unique nutritional profile and functional properties.

Plasma, obtained from blood, is rich in beneficial proteins such as albumin and globulins. Due to its rich amino acid profile, plasma is a sustainable and valuable ingredient.

## How is Animal Plasma Made for the Pet Food Industry?

The process of creating animal plasma for use in cat food involves several steps:

**Collection and Processing**

Animal plasma is obtained during the slaughter process of pigs and cattle. These animals are primarily processed for human consumption, and the plasma is a valuable by-product. After the animals are slaughtered, their blood is collected and immediately treated with an anticoagulant to prevent clotting. The blood is then centrifuged to separate the plasma from the cellular components (red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets).

**Spray-Drying Technology**

The liquid plasma undergoes spray drying, which is crucial for preserving its nutritional value. This process involves:

1. Atomization: The liquid plasma is sprayed into a fine mist.

2. Hot Air Exposure: The mist is exposed to hot air at temperatures typically above 80 °C.

3. Rapid Dehydration: The water content evaporates quickly, leaving behind dried plasma particles.

4. Collection: The dried particles are collected as a powder.

This technology is essential as it:

- Maintains the solubility of the plasma

- Preserves the nutritional value

- Retains the nutritional qualities of the plasma protein

- Eliminates potential pathogens, enhancing the safety of the final product

## What is Spray-Dried Animal Plasma?

Spray-Dried Animal Plasma (SDAP) is a highly functional ingredient derived from animal blood and plays a significant role in dry cat food formulations. This protein-rich powder provides multiple benefits beyond its nutritional value, enhancing the texture, palatability, and overall quality of dry cat food.

**Production Process**

SDAP is produced through a sophisticated spray-drying technology:

1. Blood plasma is separated from whole blood through centrifugation.

2. The liquid plasma is then atomized into fine droplets.

3. These droplets are exposed to hot air, typically at temperatures above 80 °C.

4. The water content evaporates rapidly, leaving behind dried plasma particles.

5. The resulting powder is collected and packaged.

This process preserves the nutritional qualities of the dried plasma protein while ensuring microbiological safety.

## Is Animal Plasma Safe for Cats?

The safety of animal plasma in kibbles has been extensively studied, and research generally supports its use as a safe and beneficial ingredient in dry food. The protein-rich compounds found in animal plasma, such as albumin and globulin, contribute to its high nutritional value and safety profile. However, as with any ingredient, there are considerations.

**Safety Profile**: Animal plasma, particularly in its spray-dried form (SDAP), is safe for cats when properly processed and incorporated into balanced diets. Several factors contribute to its safety profile:

**Processing Methods**: Spray drying (SD) technology produces SDAP by applying high temperatures that effectively eliminate potential pathogens. This process ensures the microbiological safety of the final product.

**Additional Safety Measures**: To further ensure safety and quality:

- A minimum post-drying storage period of 14 days at 20 °C is often implemented.

- Some manufacturers incorporate additional biosafety measures, such as UV-C irradiation, which has been shown to inactivate certain viruses in liquid plasma before spray-drying.

**Quality Control**: SDAP undergoes rigorous quality control to ensure it meets standards for pet food. This includes testing for nutritional content, microbial contamination, and potential contaminants.

The **Association of American Feed Control Officials (AAFCO)** also sets nutritional guidelines for ingredients used in pet food. While AAFCO doesn’t directly regulate pet food, it provides definitions and standards that help guide manufacturers, ensuring that ingredients like animal plasma meet industry-wide expectations for safety and animal nutrition.

### Potential Risks and Considerations

While generally safe, there are some considerations regarding the use of animal plasma in dry cat foods:

**Allergies**: As with any protein source, some cats may develop allergies to animal plasma. However, allergic reactions to animal plasma are rare compared with more common allergens such as fish or chicken.

**Source and Quality**: The safety of animal plasma can be influenced by its source and processing quality. Reputable pet food manufacturers source animal plasma from regulated facilities and implement strict quality-control measures.

**Balanced Diet**: Animal plasma should be used in balanced diets. Excessive use of any ingredient, including animal plasma, could lead to nutritional imbalances.

**Research Support**: Several studies have demonstrated the safety and health benefits of animal plasma in cat diets. Research in animal nutrition has shown that dietary plasma proteins can positively affect intestinal health and immune function in various animal species, including cats.

### Is Blood Plasma a by-product?

Blood plasma is classified as a by-product in pet food ingredients because it's not part of the primary meat cuts for human consumption. Instead, it's a component derived from animal blood, typically collected during the meat process. Key points:

- Blood plasma is obtained as a secondary product during meat processing.

- It's separated from the main meat products intended for human consumption.

- The use of blood plasma in pet food allows for the efficient utilization of animal resources.

While the term "by-product" is sometimes perceived negatively, it's important to note that blood plasma can be a valuable ingredient in dry cat food. It provides a high-quality source of protein and other nutrients that contribute to a cat's diet.

### Is Spray-Dried Animal Plasma a by-product?
